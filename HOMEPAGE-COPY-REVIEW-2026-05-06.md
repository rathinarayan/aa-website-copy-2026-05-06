# Homepage copy review

Date: 2026-05-06

## Test setup

This review treats the homepage as if it is being read by three likely buyers:

1. A founder who wants more qualified B2B conversations but does not want to build an outbound team.
2. A general manager who has tried lists, email tools or agencies and does not trust generic campaigns.
3. A growth leader who needs to justify why AA is worth a conversation.

## Grunt test

| Question | Result | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| What does AA do? | Pass | The hero now says AA helps companies reach specific B2B decision-makers. |
| Who is it for? | Pass | The page names Australian B2B companies with specific buyers to reach. |
| Why should the buyer care? | Pass | The page connects AA to qualified conversations, better buyer clarity and less reliance on referrals. |
| Why is AA different? | Pass with watch item | The difference is clear: buyer clarity, checked data, human judgement, Australian B2B buyer patterns, founding-team recognition. The award wording still needs final confirmation before launch. |
| What should the visitor do next? | Pass | The primary CTA is clear: Start a fit conversation. |

## Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Clarity | 4.5/5 | The homepage now explains the offer in plain language. |
| Business-owner value | 4/5 | The value is visible, especially around reaching buyers without building an outbound team. |
| Credibility | 4/5 | Awards and aggregate proof are now visible early. Final award wording and logo permission remain launch blockers. |
| Jargon control | 4/5 | Most internal phrases were removed. Avoid reintroducing terms like proof layer, ICP, signal, route or motion. |
| StoryBrand flow | 4.5/5 | Buyer problem, guide credibility, plan, offer and CTA are in the right order. |
| Commercial confidence | 3.5/5 | The homepage explains why AA is different. A stronger commercial proof point can be added once approved, for example ninety-day proof of value or an audited outcome metric. |

Overall score: 24.5/30

Verdict: revise one more time after award and proof claims are confirmed, but the homepage is now directionally strong.

## Fixes already applied

- Replaced internal proof language with business-owner language.
- Changed "historical buyer profiles structured" to "buyer profiles analysed".
- Changed "role groups mapped" to "decision-maker groups clarified".
- Removed the internal award-confirmation note from public homepage copy.
- Changed "AI-assisted voice" to "voice follow-up where appropriate".
- Changed "proof layer" to "proof across niches" or "proof".

## Launch watch items

- Confirm exact award wording and whether logos can be used.
- Confirm whether the homepage can say "thousands of Australian businesses since 2021".
- Confirm whether "ninety-day proof of value" should return to the homepage as a commercial confidence point.
- Confirm whether aggregate proof numbers can be public: 82 buyer profiles, 36 niches, 42 decision-maker groups and 4,821 job-title patterns.

## Recommendation

Keep the awards strip directly under the hero. It gives immediate credibility before the visitor reaches the explanation sections. Keep the wording as founding-team recognition unless the team confirms that the awards can be claimed directly by AA.
