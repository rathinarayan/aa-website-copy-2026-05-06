Pages reviewed:

- Home
- Growth
- Data
- AI Solutions
- Proof
- Team
- Contact
- Privacy
- Terms
- 404

Review lens:

- Would a normal business owner understand the page without internal AA context?
- Does the page explain what AA does, who it helps and why it matters?
- Does the page avoid jargon, internal shorthand and vendor-style language?
- Does the page keep StoryBrand flow: buyer problem, AA as guide, clear plan, proof and next step?
- Are placeholders hidden from public view and captured as launch checks instead?

## Fixes applied in this QA pass

- Removed public-facing placeholder notes from Growth, Data, AI Solutions, Proof and Contact.
- Normalised aggregate proof language across Home, Data and Proof.
- Replaced "historical buyer profiles structured" with "buyer profiles analysed".
- Replaced "niche profiles mapped" with "B2B niches mapped" where the visitor does not need internal data language.
- Replaced "role groups" with "decision-maker groups".
- Simplified AI Solutions implementation wording from system/tooling language to practical business language.
- Cleaned browser titles on Team and Contact.
- Corrected footer navigation order on Team and Contact.

## Launch checks still required

- Confirm exact award wording for BRW, Deloitte, EY, Fast 100 and Fast Starters.
- Confirm whether award logos can be used.
- Confirm public approval for the aggregate proof numbers: 82 buyer profiles, 36 niches, 42 decision-maker groups and 4,821 job-title patterns.
- Confirm whether the site can say "thousands of Australian businesses since 2021".
- Confirm whether "ninety-day proof of value" should appear on the homepage and Growth page.
- Confirm booking link and routing.
- Confirm ABN, LinkedIn URL and final legal wording.

## Overall verdict

The site is now coherent as a copy-first website. It passes the business-owner clarity test. The remaining work is not rewriting, it is claim approval: awards, aggregate numbers, legal details and booking flow.
