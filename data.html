Why this matters

A bought list can look impressive and still miss the buyer.

Job titles are messy. Industries are broad. Websites are outdated. The right buyer may not have the title everyone expects. That is why AA agrees the buyer first, then builds the contact list.

We do not buy a database and call it a strategy.

We use available public, commercial and client-approved sources to build a list around your agreed buyer, then keep improving it as replies and conversations show what the market cares about.

How we build data

Four steps before a message goes out.

This is the work most people skip. It is also why the first conversation is more likely to be with the right person.

1

Define the market.

Industries, company types, regions, buying triggers and poor-fit exclusions.

2

Map the roles.

We group messy job titles into practical buyer roles, so the outreach is aimed at the right level.

3

Build the list.

Companies and contacts are sourced against the agreed buyer and checked before campaign use.

4

Add context.

We add the details that help the first message feel relevant, not random.

Us vs a list

The difference is buyer understanding, not list size.

A generic list

  • Broad titles copied from a template
  • Single-source data with limited context
  • People included because the title matched, not because the role matters
  • Little clarity on who should be excluded
  • You learn it is wrong after the market ignores it

AA-built buyer data

  • Mapped around your specific buyer
  • Built from multiple sources depending on the campaign
  • Decision-makers grouped by authority, relevance and likely problem
  • Exclusions written clearly before extraction begins
  • Refined as replies, conversations and objections come back
