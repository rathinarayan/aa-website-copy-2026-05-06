# Copy debate decision

Date: 2026-05-06

## Method

Three-agent review:

- Advocate: defend the current site flow and identify what should be preserved.
- Critic: attack the copy from the lens of a sceptical Australian B2B business owner.
- Judge: decide the best copy and flow without inventing unapproved proof.

## Decision

Preserve the current site architecture:

Home -> Growth -> Data -> AI Solutions -> Proof -> Team -> Contact.

The flow is coherent and explains the business in plain English. The issue was not structure. The issue was commercial sharpness and claim risk.

## Implemented now

- Changed the primary CTA from "Start a fit conversation" to "Start the conversation".
- Changed the Contact form button to "Send your context".
- Removed "performance-aligned" from public copy until the commercial model is approved.
- Removed early AI language from the homepage hero so the main offer stays outbound-first.
- Reframed the Proof page as buyer-mapping depth, not outcome proof.
- Softened awards wording to founder recognition connected to prior Australian growth businesses.
- Tightened Team language so India operations supports quality and management standards.
- Reworded AI Solutions examples around time saved, fewer handoffs and clearer reporting.
- Made the Contact form note feel deliberate rather than temporary.

## Deferred until approved

- Named client case studies.
- Revenue, ROI, meeting, conversion or sales pipeline claims.
- Ninety-day proof of value.
- "Thousands of Australian businesses since 2021."
- Exact award names, logos, finalist status and whether AA or only the founding team can claim them.
- Any wording around risk share, guarantees, success fees or performance pricing.
- Booking link or embedded scheduler.

## Final judgement

The best copy direction is not another full rewrite. It is a tighter version of the current structure: outbound-first, buyer-clear, proof-honest, founder-credible and easy to act on.
